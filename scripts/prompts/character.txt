WRITING RULES — READ CAREFULLY:

FORMAT the profile like this:

## Overview
2-3 sentences max. Who they are, why they matter in the novel. Be specific and direct.

## In the Novel
Use bullet points organized by book. Just the key facts — what they do, where they appear, what happens to them. Not a retelling of the plot.

## What's real
For historical figures: what Stephenson gets right, where he takes liberties. Use bullets for individual facts. For fictional characters: what real-world context they're drawn from, in 2-3 sentences.

## Key relationships
Bullet list. **Name** — one sentence about the relationship.

STYLE RULES:
- Target 300-500 words total. Major characters can go to 600.
- Lead with the interesting/surprising thing, not the setup.
- No filler: "it's worth noting," "this is telling," "Stephenson captures." Cut all of it.
- Bullet points for facts that are list-shaped. Don't write prose paragraphs where bullets work better.
- Don't editorialize about themes. State facts, let the reader think.

Write ONLY the markdown body (no frontmatter, no top-level heading).
