English architect, astronomer, and mathematician (1632-1723).

## Life and Work

Before he was the architect of St Paul's Cathedral, Wren was a professor of astronomy at Oxford and one of the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society)'s leading mathematicians. He made contributions to optics, mechanics, and the study of curves.

The [Great Fire of London](/topic/great-fire-of-london) (1666) gave him his second career. He designed 51 London churches and oversaw the rebuilding of St Paul's, a project that took 35 years. His close collaborator on many building projects was [Robert Hooke](/topic/robert-hooke), who served as City Surveyor.

Wren was a founding member of the Royal Society and one of the few people of the era who moved easily between mathematics, science, and practical engineering.

## In the Novel

Wren is part of [Daniel](/topic/daniel-waterhouse)'s circle of natural philosophers in London, appearing alongside Hooke and other Royal Society figures.
