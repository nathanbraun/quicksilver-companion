## Overview

Earl of Epsom and Lord Chancellor. The Tory half of the Comstock family — political fixer, Anglican establishment man, architect of the Act of Uniformity. One of the fictional CABAL ministers Stephenson weaves into the real Restoration power structure.

## In the Novel

- **Book 1** — First mentioned as a powerful courtier alongside [Thomas More Anglesey](/topic/thomas-more-anglesey). Drafts the Act of Uniformity, which forces all English clergy to conform to Anglican worship — devastating for [Puritans](/topic/puritans) like Drake Waterhouse and [Knott Bolstrood](/topic/knott-bolstrood).
- Comstock House, his great compound along Piccadilly near St. James's Street, represents the wealth and political power of [Restoration](/topic/restoration-london) aristocracy.
- His cousin [Roger Comstock](/topic/roger-comstock) takes the opposite political path — Whig to John's Tory.

## What's real

Entirely fictional. Stephenson's own annotation: "Don't bother Googling them." John Comstock borrows traits from several real figures — **John Evelyn** (the diarist and early [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society) figure) and **Edward Hyde, Earl of Clarendon** ([Charles II](/topic/charles-ii)'s chief advisor and actual architect of the Clarendon Code, of which the Act of Uniformity was part). Comstock House is adapted from Clarendon's actual residence, Clarendon House, built on Piccadilly in the 1660s and demolished in 1683.

## The CABAL

Stephenson's fictional CABAL — **C**omstock, **A**nglesey, **B**olstrood, **A**pthorpe, **L**ewis — mirrors the historical CABAL ministry of [Charles II](/topic/charles-ii) (**C**lifford, **A**rlington, **B**uckingham, **A**shley, **L**auderdale). Like the real CABAL, this group spans the political spectrum, united mainly by proximity to the king.
