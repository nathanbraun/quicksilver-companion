## Overview

The most enigmatic figure in the Baroque Cycle. Appears in the opening scene on Boston Common, 1713. Also appeared in *Cryptonomicon*, set in the 1940s and 1990s, apparently unaged. Whether he's immortal, an alchemist who found the secret of eternal life, or something else, Stephenson never says.

## In the Novel

- **1713 chapters** — Arrives in Boston seeking [Daniel Waterhouse](/topic/daniel-waterhouse), carrying a summons from [Princess Caroline](/topic/caroline-of-ansbach). Sets the entire plot in motion.
- **1670** — Appears at a [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society) meeting, seeming to know everyone.
- **1684** — Turns up in Saxony alongside [Jack](/topic/jack-shaftoe), [Eliza](/topic/eliza), and [Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz).
- **1687** — Present at the Hague for conversations about William of Orange's planned invasion.
- Each appearance raises more questions. He knows things he shouldn't. His connections span continents and decades. The timeline of a normal life can't accommodate all of his appearances.

## What's real

Wholly fictional and deliberately impossible. No historical figure maps onto him. His name is significant: the biblical Enoch "walked with God" and was taken up without dying (Genesis 5:24) — one of the few biblical figures associated with immortality. If Enoch has achieved what [Newton](/topic/isaac-newton) spent decades pursuing in his [alchemical](/topic/alchemy) lab, that's the novel's deepest irony.

## Key relationships

- **[Daniel Waterhouse](/topic/daniel-waterhouse)** — His summoner and companion aboard the *Minerva*.
- **[Newton](/topic/isaac-newton) and [Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz)** — Seems to know both personally, with a familiarity predating Daniel's involvement with either.
- **[Jack](/topic/jack-shaftoe) and [Eliza](/topic/eliza)** — Appears alongside them in Book 2, suggesting he's monitoring all three protagonists.
- In *Cryptonomicon*, he appears as a WWII cryptographer and again in the 1990s, unchanged — the only character spanning Stephenson's full shared universe.
