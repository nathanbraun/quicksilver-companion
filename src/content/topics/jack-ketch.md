England's official executioner from 1663 to 1686.

## History

Ketch became notorious for botching executions. His most infamous failure was the beheading of the Duke of [Monmouth](/topic/duke-of-monmouth) in 1685 — it took him five blows with the axe and possibly a finishing stroke with a knife. He reportedly apologized beforehand, saying he was nervous. The crowd nearly lynched him.

His name became a byword. "Jack Ketch" entered the language as a generic term for any executioner or hangman, and he became the hangman character in Punch and Judy puppet shows, a role that persisted for centuries.

## In the novel

Ketch is a real presence at the executions the characters witness. For [Bob Shaftoe](/topic/bob-shaftoe) and others who live near the margins of the law, he is not an abstraction but a specific person they might one day face on the scaffold.
