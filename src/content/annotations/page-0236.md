Daniel returns to Cambridge, and the cultural upheaval of Restoration theater echoes even in the university.

**"Once More Into the Breeches"** — A pun on Shakespeare's "Once more unto the breach, dear friends" (*Henry V*, Act III) and on [Restoration theater's](/topic/restoration-theater) popular "breeches roles," in which actresses wore men's clothing on stage. Stephenson's annotation: "Readers who enjoy this can find much more in the same vein under the rubric of Restoration Plays." Breeches roles were wildly popular — they allowed audiences to see women's legs, which was otherwise impossible in an era of floor-length skirts. The plays of this period delighted in sexual double entendres and gender confusion, in deliberate contrast to the [Puritan](/topic/puritans) era that had banned theater entirely.
