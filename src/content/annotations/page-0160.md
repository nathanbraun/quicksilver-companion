The Great Fire of London and the king's surprising personal involvement.

**"Regent's firewall"** — [Charles II](/topic/charles-ii) personally fought the [Great Fire of London](/topic/great-fire-of-london) in September 1666, working alongside his brother James to direct firefighters, finance relief, provide food and shelter, and — crucially — order houses blown up to create firebreaks. Will Durant confirms this in *The Story of Civilization*: "both the King and his brother James labored with their own hands among the firefighters." The image of a king personally demolishing buildings to save his city struck the original wiki annotator as too bizarre to be fiction, but it's well documented. The fire destroyed 13,200 houses, 87 churches, and old St. Paul's Cathedral over four days — but the death toll was remarkably low, probably under 10.
