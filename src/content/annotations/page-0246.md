The legal world of Restoration England and the men who wielded its power.

**"Apthorp — the second A"** — Stephenson's annotation: "Apthorp is fictional, but, like many of the other fictional characters in the book, representative of a certain type. Jeffreys really existed and by all accounts really was this noxious." [Judge George Jeffreys](/topic/judge-jeffreys) (1645–1689) was the Lord Chief Justice under [James II](/topic/james-ii), infamous for the "Bloody Assizes" of 1685 in which he sentenced hundreds of [Monmouth](/topic/monmouth) Rebellion participants to death or transportation. Jeffreys was renowned for his cruelty, his drunkenness, and his willingness to serve as a judicial instrument of royal tyranny. He died in the Tower of London after the [Glorious Revolution](/topic/glorious-revolution).
