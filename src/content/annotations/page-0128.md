Newton's obsessive experimental habits are on full display.

**"Fly stuck to quill"** — Stephenson's annotation. Newton's meticulous, compulsive approach to experimentation extended to recording the tiniest details. He famously stuck a bodkin (a blunt needle) behind his own eyeball to study how pressure affected his perception of color — an experiment he recorded with clinical detachment. The image of a fly stuck to his quill captures both his intense focus and his tendency to notice and catalog everything, no matter how trivial. This obsessiveness would serve him well in optics and mathematics; it would also fuel his paranoia, his grudges, and his decades-long pursuit of [alchemical](/topic/alchemy) secrets that led nowhere.
