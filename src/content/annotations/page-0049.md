Daniel heads to Cambridge as England's religious landscape shifts violently.

**Act of Uniformity** — The night before Daniel rides to Cambridge, [Charles II](/topic/charles-ii) signs the Act of Uniformity (1662), requiring all ministers to use the Book of Common Prayer and accept episcopal ordination. In one stroke, nearly 2,000 of England's 9,000 clergymen — including all [Puritan](/topic/puritans) and Independent ministers — became "unemployed heretics." Daniel's father [Drake](/topic/drake-waterhouse), as a committed Dissenter, is directly targeted. The Act was part of the Clarendon Code, a suite of laws (Corporation Act, Conventicle Act, Five-Mile Act) designed to crush nonconformism. Dissenters were also barred from degrees at Oxford and Cambridge — making Daniel's presence there politically charged from day one.

**[John Comstock](/topic/john-comstock)** — Stephenson's fictional courtier who drafts the Act. He stands in for the real Earl of Clarendon (Edward Hyde), Charles II's chief advisor. The fictional CABAL ministry — **C**omstock, [**A**nglesey](/topic/thomas-more-anglesey), [**B**olstrood](/topic/knott-bolstrood), **A**pthorp, **L**ewis — mirrors the historical CABAL of **C**lifford, **A**rlington, **B**uckingham, **A**shley, **L**auderdale.
