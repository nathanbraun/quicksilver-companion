Enoch sketches the ten-year-old Newton.

**The muse** — Enoch needs inspiration to draw the boy. In Greek mythology, the nine Muses were daughters of Zeus and Mnemosyne (Memory) — goddesses of the arts and sciences. That Enoch invokes a pagan concept so casually tells us something about him: he's not a [Puritan](/topic/puritans), not bound by the religious strictures that define everyone else in this world. The scene also quietly establishes that Enoch recognizes something extraordinary in Newton before anyone else does — he's already documenting the boy, as if for posterity.
