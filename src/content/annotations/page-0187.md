Samuel Pepys appears at the Royal Society, bringing his diarist's eye to the proceedings.

**"Samuel Pepys"** — [Pepys](/topic/samuel-pepys) (1633–1703) is one of history's most valuable witnesses, not because he was important (though he became so), but because he wrote everything down. His diary, kept from 1660 to 1669, records the Restoration, the [Great Plague](/topic/plague), the [Great Fire](/topic/great-fire-of-london), his work at the Navy Office, his sex life, his meals, and his conversations with an almost pathological thoroughness. He was elected a Fellow of the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society) in 1665 and later served as its president (1684–1686). His diary can be read in its entirety at pepysdiary.com, which presents each entry on its anniversary date with crowdsourced annotations — making it, as the original wiki notes, a kind of metaweb itself.
