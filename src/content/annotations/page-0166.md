Pirate schooners appear on the horizon and the Minerva's crew springs into action.

**"Schooners"** — Stephenson's own annotation: "The word 'schooner' appears to have been coined in New England in 1713. By using it Dappa is showing off what a hip guy he is." A schooner is a fore-and-aft rigged vessel with two or more masts, faster and more maneuverable than the square-rigged ships of the line that dominated naval warfare. Their speed made them ideal for [piracy](/topic/pirates) and smuggling.

**"Teach"** — [Edward Teach, a.k.a. Blackbeard](/topic/blackbeard), the most famous pirate of the era. Stephenson's annotation: "Blackbeard is mostly known as a pirate along the North Carolina coast who was most active a few years later (circa 1717). But pirates were obviously quite mobile. Teach was not unknown in Boston and it's plausible to put him here in 1713." Teach created his terrifying persona deliberately — he tucked lit slow-burning hemp cord under his hat to wreathe himself in smoke during battle, wore six pistols, and braided his enormous beard with colored ribbons. Ships often surrendered at the sight of him.

**"Flashes of light"** — Heliography — signaling with reflected sunlight — predates the telegraph by centuries. Mirrors or polished metal surfaces could transmit simple messages over long distances on clear days. The technique was used by ancient Greeks and Romans and remained militarily relevant into the 19th century. The flashes Daniel observes are part of the Minerva crew's surveillance of approaching vessels.

**"Strange information"** — Stephenson's annotation: "This phrase 'strange information' was employed by me in *Cryptonomicon* as homage to Suzanne Vega. It has somewhat taken on a life of its own." It's from her song "Bad Wisdom" off the 1992 album *99.9 F°*.
