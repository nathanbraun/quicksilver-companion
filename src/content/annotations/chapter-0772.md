Eliza writes to Leibniz from the court of Versailles, detailing her sudden elevation to the nobility and the political maneuvers of the French court.

**"SOPHIE"** — [Sophia of the Palatinate](/topic/hanoverian-succession) was the Electress of Hanover and a formidable intellectual who maintained a lifelong correspondence with [Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz](/topic/leibniz). As the granddaughter of James I, she was eventually named heir presumptive to the British throne, making her the link between the Stuarts and the modern House of Windsor.


**"Sghr"** — Stephenson's annotation: "[Qwghlm](/topic/qwghlm) is a region of the British Isles composed of two islands. On this page, Eliza's letter names one as [Sghr](/topic/sghr), leading me to wonder what native name the other island might have." The name refers to the craggy, unpronounceable geography of the fictional islands, which serve as a recurring setting in Stephenson's work.


**"the Qwghlmian Church... founded before the Roman Catholic Church was established"** — This reflects the historical existence of "Celtic Christianity," which developed in the British Isles somewhat independently of Rome before the Synod of Whitby in 664. [Father Édouard de Gex](/topic/edouard-de-gex) seeks to exploit these ancient origins to claim the islands for the Catholic interest.

**"Louis XIV"** — Known as the "Sun King," [Louis XIV](/topic/louis-xiv) transformed France into the dominant European power through absolute monarchy and a series of aggressive wars. His court at [Versailles](/topic/versailles) was designed to domesticate the nobility by forcing them to compete for his favor through elaborate rituals.
