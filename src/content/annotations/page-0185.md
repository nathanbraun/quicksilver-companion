Music and medicine collide in a Royal Society demonstration.

**"Tarantella"** — The tarantella is a rapid Southern Italian folk dance traditionally believed to cure the bite of the tarantula spider. Victims of *tarantismus* — a condition documented from the 15th century onward — would dance for hours or days, supposedly sweating out the venom. Modern scholars believe tarantismus was likely a form of mass psychogenic illness unrelated to actual spider bites (tarantula bites are not medically serious). The dance became a popular folk tradition independent of its supposed medical purpose. That the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society) would discuss it is perfectly in character: the Society's early meetings covered everything from blood transfusions to the mating habits of insects, all in the same spirit of empirical curiosity.
