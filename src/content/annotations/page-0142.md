The natural philosophers turn their attention to the geometries hidden in nature.

**"Symmetry of snowflakes"** — Johannes Kepler was the first to ask why snowflakes always have six-fold symmetry, in his 1611 pamphlet *Strena Seu de Nive Sexangula* (A New Year's Gift of Hexagonal Snow). He couldn't explain it — the molecular structure of ice wouldn't be understood for centuries — but he correctly intuited that the regularity must arise from the packing of tiny identical units. [Robert Hooke](/topic/robert-hooke) illustrated snowflakes in *Micrographia* (1665), confirming their hexagonal structure under the microscope. The question of why natural forms exhibit mathematical regularity — from snowflakes to honeycombs to the spiral of a nautilus shell — is exactly the kind of puzzle that drove the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society)'s experimental program.
