[Drake Waterhouse](/topic/drake-waterhouse) and Cromwell's religious conversion.

**"Found God"** — Stephenson's annotation: "[Cromwell](/topic/oliver-cromwell) apparently did undergo a religious conversion along these lines. I have inserted the fictitious Drake Waterhouse into the historical narrative at this point." Oliver Cromwell's transformation from minor country gentleman to God's instrument of revolution is one of the [English Civil War](/topic/english-civil-war)'s great mysteries. Sometime in the late 1620s or early 1630s, Cromwell experienced a profound spiritual crisis and emerged as a fervent [Puritan](/topic/puritans), convinced that God spoke to him directly. By placing Drake at Cromwell's side during this conversion, Stephenson gives the Waterhouse family a founding myth — they were present at the moment England's future Lord Protector decided he was doing God's work. Drake's radical Puritanism, which will shape Daniel's entire childhood, begins here.
