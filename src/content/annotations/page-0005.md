Enoch continues through Boston, observing fire scars and a ranting preacher.

**"Seen more than one city burn"** — The 1711 fire destroyed a third of Boston, including the meeting house. The Old State House being built to replace it was constructed in 1713 — the year of this scene. The phrase also quietly raises a question: how many cities has Enoch watched burn, exactly? If he witnessed the [Great Fire of London](/topic/great-fire-of-london) in 1666 and the [plague](/topic/plague) that preceded it, his count is already impressive.

**"The last one"** — Stephenson's own annotation, in full: "The last hanging of a supposed witch in New England occurred at around this time, so he's not far off." Salem was only twenty years ago.

**The preacher** — Annotator Alan Sinder guessed this is Cotton Mather — Harvard man, prolific author, central figure in the Salem witch trials, and known to preach at pirate executions in Boston. Daniel's nephew Wait-Still Waterhouse also went to Harvard. Whether or not it's specifically Mather, the type is right.
