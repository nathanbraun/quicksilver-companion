The scene shifts to Epsom as Daniel and Isaac retreat from plague-stricken London into the countryside.

**"At Epsom"** — Stephenson's annotation: this section is set at Epsom, in Surrey, where many Londoners fled to escape the [plague](/topic/plague). Epsom was already known for its mineral springs (Epsom salts — magnesium sulfate — were first distilled from the town's spring water in 1618). The retreat to Epsom parallels [Newton](/topic/isaac-newton)'s famous retreat to Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire during the plague years — his *annus mirabilis* — when he developed early ideas about [calculus](/topic/calculus-priority-dispute), optics, and gravitation. Stephenson places Daniel alongside Newton during this transformative period, making Daniel witness to the birth of modern [natural philosophy](/topic/natural-philosophy).
