Enoch walks through autumn Boston. The narrative notes the temperature using two scales.

**[Newton](/topic/isaac-newton)'s temperature scale** — Real and wonderfully obscure. Around 1700, Newton ranked temperatures using linseed oil: 0 for freezing water, 12 for body heat, 33 for boiling water. A perfectly usable scale — just not the one that stuck.

**Fahrenheit's thermometers** — "Herr Fahrenheit" and his "new [quicksilver](/topic/quicksilver-mercury)-filled, sealed-tube thermometers." Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686-1736) was perfecting mercury thermometers right around this period. Mercury was ideal: expands uniformly, doesn't stick to glass, stays liquid across a wide range. His scale (32 freezing, 212 boiling) would become the English-speaking standard.

**"Pale gray eyes"** — Enoch's eyes. Community annotator Andux noted this is a trait of the goddess Athena in Homer — "gray-eyed Athena." Make of that what you will.
