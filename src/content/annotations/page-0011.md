Two annotations for this page that turn out to be connected.

**"Gold knows things that no man does"** — Newton says this to Enoch at the London docks. Newton spent decades on [alchemy](/topic/alchemy) — over a million words, more than on any other subject. His appointment as Warden of the Royal Mint in 1696 was not ironic to him; it was a natural extension of his obsession with the nature of matter. He pursued counterfeiters through London's underworld with the same intensity he brought to optics. The alchemical symbol for gold also appears on the cover of *Cryptonomicon*.

**Hypothesis of Vortices** — Stephenson's own annotation: Descartes and others (including [Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz), for a time) tried to explain planetary orbits through vortices in the aether — invisible whirlpools physically carrying planets along. Stephenson: this "seems nutty to us, but was preferable to them, on metaphysical grounds, to the notion of action at a distance, which is a bit troublesome even today." Newton's gravity described *what* happened but not *why*. How does the Sun pull on the Earth across empty space? Vortices offered a mechanical answer. Newton offered none — hence *hypotheses non fingo*.
