Optical phenomena prompt a meditation on perception and illusion.

**"The sun becomes an oval"** — Atmospheric refraction does flatten the sun and moon near the horizon, compressing them vertically into an oval shape. But the more dramatic effect — the apparent enlargement of the sun and moon at the horizon — is mostly an optical illusion. The "Moon Illusion" has puzzled scientists since Aristotle. The leading explanation involves the Ponzo Illusion: the brain perceives the sky as a flattened dome rather than a true hemisphere, making objects at the apparently "farther" horizon seem larger than they do at the zenith. But no single explanation fully accounts for the effect. The fact that a simple everyday observation — the setting sun looking bigger — resists complete scientific explanation connects to the novel's theme that [natural philosophy](/topic/natural-philosophy) constantly reveals how little we understand about even the most familiar phenomena.
