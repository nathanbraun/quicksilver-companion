Enoch wanders through Boston's streets past chandleries and tanneries.

**"Bits of slaughtered animals"** — Tanning was among the foulest trades of the era: urine-soaked hides, dung for softening, the stench bad enough to banish tanneries to town edges. Stephenson doesn't sanitize the past.

**"Cartesian number-line"** — Stephenson's own annotation is a gem: "Cartesian coordinates" is actually a misnomer. Descartes used number-lines to map space, but the practice of crossing them at right angles "appears to have originated elsewhere." Julian Barbour credits [Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz). Stephenson called it "a fascinating topic, but murky enough that I have avoided making a big deal out of it in the novel." Even something as basic as a coordinate system has a disputed origin — a preview of the [calculus priority fight](/topic/calculus-priority-dispute) to come.
