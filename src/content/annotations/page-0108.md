The colonial connections of England's Puritan network come into view.

**"Mayflower"** — The Mayflower carried 102 passengers from Plymouth, England to Plymouth, Massachusetts in 1620. About half were Separatist [Puritans](/topic/puritans) (the "Pilgrims"), and roughly half were other colonists the venture's financial backers had recruited. The ship was a standard merchant vessel of about 180 tons, not purpose-built for colonization. The voyage took 66 days. Within a year, half the passengers were dead from disease and exposure. The Waterhouse family's roots in this Puritan migration network — connecting old England to New England — are central to Daniel's identity and to the novel's transatlantic geography.
