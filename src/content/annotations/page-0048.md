Daniel witnesses a cathedral being demolished.

**Demolishing the cathedral** — Not vandalism but policy. During and after the [English Civil War](/topic/english-civil-war), [Puritan](/topic/puritans) forces systematically destroyed or repurposed Anglican and Catholic religious buildings. Cathedrals were defaced, stained glass smashed, organs ripped out, and some structures demolished entirely. [Cromwell](/topic/oliver-cromwell)'s soldiers stabled horses in nave aisles. The destruction reflected a theological conviction: elaborate churches were idolatrous, distracting from the pure Word. For young Daniel, raised in this world, watching a cathedral come down is just Tuesday. The fuller context for this demolition is explained around page 270.
