Daniel boards the *Minerva* and Stephenson addresses the ship question.

**Lascars** — The *Minerva*'s crew includes lascars — sailors from the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. The term comes from the Persian *lashkar* (army/camp). By 1713, lascars were common on European trading vessels, especially those of the Dutch and English East India Companies. They were typically hired at lower wages than European sailors and often served under brutal conditions. Captain van Hoek, who built the *Minerva* in India from Malabar teak (more rot-resistant than European oak), would naturally have recruited Indian crew.

**"Not a Ship Novel"** — Stephenson's own annotation: "Now that we are on board a ship with Daniel, it's as good a place as any to mention that this is not a Ship Novel. I was tempted to make it one, but bated because I knew at some level that to plunge into that lore would make the book twice as long even as it already was." Given that *Quicksilver* is already 927 pages, the restraint is noted. He puts in "enough Ship stuff that the novel will hold water, as it were" — a pun he clearly enjoyed.
