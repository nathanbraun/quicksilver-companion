Jack and Eliza travel through the fragmented territories of the Holy Roman Empire toward the Dutch Republic, witnessing the immense scale of global trade and the defensive waterworks of the Netherlands.

**"the onslaught on Vienna"** — The [Battle of Vienna (1683)](/topic/battle-of-vienna) saw a coalition of European forces break the Ottoman Empire's siege of the city, effectively halting Ottoman expansion into Central Europe just a year before this chapter begins.

**"King Looie—not satisfied with bombarding Genoa, laying siege to Luxembourg"** — [Louis XIV](/topic/louis-xiv) of France, the "Sun King," pursued an aggressive policy of "Reunions," claiming and seizing border territories to expand French influence throughout the 1680s.

**"challenging Pope Innocent XI to a staredown"** — [Pope Innocent XI](/topic/pope-innocent-xi) had a notoriously hostile relationship with Louis XIV, primarily over the "Gallican Liberties," which sought to increase the French crown's control over the Church at the expense of papal authority.

**"expelling Jewry from Bordeaux"** — This reflects the escalating religious persecution under Louis XIV that culminated in the 1685 Edict of Fontainebleau; many [expelled Jews](/topic/expulsion-of-jews-france) and Huguenots fled to the more tolerant Dutch Republic.

**"hordes of unemployed men going to look for work in Dutch cities—Holland-gänger"** — [Hollandgänger](/topic/holland-ganger) were seasonal migrant workers, mostly from impoverished German states, who traveled to the [Dutch Republic](/topic/dutch-republic) to perform grueling labor like peat cutting or hay harvesting for higher wages.

**"lines of circumvallation... walls, ditches, ramparts, palisades, moats"** — These defenses were part of the [Dutch Water Line](/topic/dutch-water-line), a sophisticated military system that used controlled flooding and fortifications to turn the heart of the Netherlands into an impregnable island.

**"growing flax, hemp, rape, hops, tobacco, woad, and madder"** — Rather than subsistence grain, the Dutch specialized in [industrial crops](/topic/dutch-industrial-crops) used for high-value exports like textiles, beer, and dyes (woad and madder).

**"bleaching bolts of English cloth in buttermilk"** — The [Dutch bleaching industry](/topic/dutch-bleaching-industry) was world-renowned; the specific chemical properties of local water combined with sour milk produced the whitest linens in Europe.

**"William of Orange had opened the sluices and flooded the land"** — During the [Rampjaar (Year of Disaster) in 1672](/topic/rampjaar-1672), the Dutch intentionally breached their dikes to create a water barrier that successfully halted the invading French army.

**"Germany (as that mess of Duchies, Electorates, Landgraviates, Margraviates, Counties, Bishoprics, Archbishoprics, and Principalities was called)"** — Before its 19th-century unification, "Germany" was the [Holy Roman Empire](/topic/holy-roman-empire), a chaotic patchwork of hundreds of semi-sovereign states with their own tolls, laws, and currencies.

**"his latest attempt to sell Kuxen"** — [Kuxen](/topic/kuxen) were a traditional German form of mining shares; Leibniz frequently sought investors for Harz Mountain silver mines to fund his scientific endeavors.

**"ready to be minted into thalers"** — The [Thaler](/topic/thaler) was a large silver coin used throughout Europe for centuries; its name is the linguistic ancestor of the modern "dollar."

**"The Jews don’t even bother to give it a name... they just call it mokum"** — [Mokum](/topic/mokum) is a Yiddish-derived nickname for Amsterdam (from the Hebrew *makom*, meaning "place"); it signifies the city's status as a safe haven for Jewish refugees.

**"barges laden with peat"** — The [Dutch peat industry](/topic/dutch-peat-industry) provided the cheap, abundant fuel that powered the Republic’s breweries, sugar refineries, and homes, serving as the "coal" of the Dutch Golden Age.

**"To write it down in a vast Encyclopedia that will be a sort of machine"** — Stephenson's annotation: "Refers to Leibniz's 'Stepped Reckoner' and his vision of a library or encyclopedia that functions as a logical calculus engine." This was a precursor to modern [computing machines](/topic/computing-machines).

**"the river Amstel, which took them into the place... where it had long ago been dammed up"** — The name [Amsterdam](/topic/amsterdam-history) literally refers to the "Dam on the Amstel," which created the central square and the city's iconic concentric canal rings.

**"The countryside was divided up into innumerable separate pays with their own tolls"** — Unlike the unified Dutch market, France was fractured into [internal customs zones](/topic/french-internal-customs) (pays), where local tolls made transporting goods prohibitively expensive.

**"The richest of those warehouse-streets was Warmoesstraat"** — [Warmoesstraat](/topic/warmoesstraat) was the commercial heart of old Amsterdam, where the wealthiest merchants lived directly above the warehouses containing their global trade goods.

**"numerous Jews among them"** — The [Jewish community of Amsterdam](/topic/jews-in-amsterdam) was largely composed of Sephardic "New Christians" fleeing the Inquisition; their capital and trade networks were vital to the city's economic dominance.

**"The landlady was a hound-faced Calvinist who had immediately recognized Eliza as one who was predestined for Hell"** — [Calvinism](/topic/calvinism-predestination) was the official religion of the Republic, centered on the doctrine of predestination—the belief that God had already chosen who would be saved or damned regardless of their earthly actions.

**"large courtyard called the Exchange"** — The [Amsterdam Stock Exchange](/topic/amsterdam-stock-exchange) was the first of its kind, where shares in the VOC were traded alongside commodities in a frantic, open-air courtyard.

**"thrown into a workhouse to be reformed"** — The [Dutch workhouses](/topic/dutch-workhouses), such as the *Rasphuis*, were famous for "rehabilitating" the poor through hard labor, such as rasping brazilwood into dye powder.

**"small letters O and C impaled on the horns of a large V"** — This was the logo of the [VOC (Dutch East India Company)](/topic/voc-dutch-east-india-company), the world's first multinational corporation, which possessed its own army, navy, and the power to wage war.

**"Exchange Bank was the same thing as the Town Hall"** — The [Wisselbank](/topic/wisselbank) (Exchange Bank) was located in the basement of the Town Hall; it provided a stable, guaranteed currency that made Amsterdam the world's financial "clearinghouse."
