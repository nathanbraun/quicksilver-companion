The political machinery behind Charles II's court reveals itself.

**"CABAL"** — Stephenson's annotation: "Charles II really did have a CABAL and it really was an acronym for the names of the leading figures (though the word 'cabal' is, of course, much older). I have changed the names and the people and their roles to suit the requirements of this book." The historical CABAL ministry (c. 1668–1674) consisted of Clifford, Arlington, Buckingham, Ashley (later Lord Shaftesbury), and Lauderdale. The word "cabal" itself derives from the Hebrew *qabbālāh* and had meant a secret political clique long before [Charles II](/topic/charles-ii)'s ministry made the acronym famous.

**"John Locke was writing a constitution for Carolina"** — [John Locke](/topic/john-locke) co-authored the *Fundamental Constitutions of Carolina* in 1669 with his patron Lord Ashley (later the Earl of Shaftesbury), one of the Lords Proprietor of the Carolina colony. The document is a strange hybrid: it established religious tolerance (remarkably progressive) while also creating a feudal aristocracy with serfs (remarkably regressive). Locke's involvement is ironic given that he would later write the *Two Treatises of Government* arguing against hereditary political authority. The Carolina constitution was never fully implemented.
