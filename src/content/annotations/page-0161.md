The tools of 17th-century violence make an appearance.

**"Blunderbuss"** — The name derives from the Dutch *donder* (thunder) and *bus* (gun). Despite popular belief, the blunderbuss's widely flared muzzle was not primarily designed to scatter shot — any short barrel does that. The flare's real purpose was to make it easier to reload from the muzzle end while standing on a bucking coach seat or rolling ship deck. Pouring loose powder and shot into a narrow tube under stress was difficult enough on solid ground; on a moving platform, the funnel-shaped opening was the difference between reloading and blowing yourself up. Tolkien quotes the Oxford English Dictionary's definition in *Farmer Giles of Ham*: "A short gun with a large bore firing many balls or slugs, and capable of doing execution within a limited range without exact aim."
