Vagabond wit and the language of the Restoration streets.

**"What is to come?"** — Eric S. Raymond (of open-source fame) notes an anachronism: Tom the Vagabond's lewd pun on "come" as a sexual term is a Victorian development, not well-attested until the 1890s. In period, the equivalent verb was "to spend" — but that would have ruined the wordplay. Stephenson occasionally accepts minor linguistic anachronisms in service of readability and humor. The novel is full of period language, but it's not a museum piece — it's a living narrative that sometimes prioritizes communication with modern readers over strict historical accuracy.
