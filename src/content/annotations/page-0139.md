The dream of encoding all knowledge into a single system takes shape.

**"Systematic alphabet"** — This refers to [John Wilkins](/topic/john-wilkins)'s project for a "Real Character" — a universal written language in which the symbol for each concept would reveal its place in a logical taxonomy of all knowledge. Wilkins published *An Essay towards a Real Character and a Philosophical Language* in 1668, classifying the entire universe into 40 genera, subdivided into differences and species. [Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz) would later build on this idea with his [Universal Characteristic](/topic/universal-characteristic), aiming to go beyond mere classification to a symbolic calculus that could mechanically generate truths. Both projects are ancestors of modern formal logic, database schemas, and programming languages — though neither worked as intended.
