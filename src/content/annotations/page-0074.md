A new chapter opens at Cambridge, 1664. A detail about the ship.

**"Wheel by which the ship is steered"** — Stephenson's annotation: "Actually most ships of this day probably didn't have wheels, because it was a relatively recent innovation; but as we'll see in future volumes, Minerva is ahead of its time in some respects." The balanced ship's wheel replaced the tiller around 1705 — likely adapted from Indian or Chinese shipbuilding technology imported via the Dutch and Portuguese. Captain van Hoek, who built the *Minerva* in India, would have had access to innovations unavailable in European dockyards. The *Minerva*'s technological edge is a running theme: she's faster and more maneuverable than her European-built competitors, which matters enormously when pirates are chasing you.
