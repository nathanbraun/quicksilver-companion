Back to 1713. Enoch's arrival gives Daniel a shock.

**Sir John Floyer** — "Sir John Floyer just invented it" — the pulse watch. Floyer (1649-1734) really did pioneer pulse measurement, publishing *The Physician's Pulse Watch* in 1707. He advocated counting the pulse for exactly one minute using a special watch. Before Floyer, checking the pulse was qualitative (fast, slow, strong, weak). He made it quantitative — one small example of the novel's central theme: turning observation into measurement — the same impulse that drives the [longitude problem](/topic/longitude).

**Enoch's pattern** — The wiki notes that Enoch nearly gives Clarke a heart attack (appearing unannounced in 1655) and nearly does the same to Daniel fifty-eight years later. His greetings always arrive like detonations — appropriate for a man carrying news that will reshape lives.
