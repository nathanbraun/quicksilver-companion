Robert Hooke enters Daniel's world, bringing the Royal Society's experimental culture with him.

**"Hooke approaches"** — Stephenson's annotation confirms this is a pivotal meeting. [Robert Hooke](/topic/robert-hooke) was curator of experiments for the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society), responsible for performing demonstrations at weekly meetings. He was the Society's workhorse — brilliant, overworked, and perpetually underappreciated compared to the gentlemen-philosophers he served. His approach to Daniel during the [plague](/topic/plague) retreat establishes a relationship that will pull Daniel into the Society's orbit and, ultimately, into the middle of the conflict between Hooke and [Newton](/topic/isaac-newton).
