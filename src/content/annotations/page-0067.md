Daniel worries about how his son will remember him.

**"Godfrey can imagine a hero-father"** — Daniel, about to sail for England at age 67, wonders what kind of father-image his young son Godfrey will construct. The wiki expands this into a meditation on the mythic hero-father archetype via Joseph Campbell's *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* and the Daedalus/Icarus myth — a father-engineer whose son dies because of the father's inventions. Daniel is no Daedalus, but he's an inventor leaving a child behind to cross an ocean in storm season. The anxiety is real: in 1713, plenty of fathers left and never came back.
