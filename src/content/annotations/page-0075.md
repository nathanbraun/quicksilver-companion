Newton's sundial experiments at Trinity.

**Sundial synchronization** — Newton marks the shadow of a sundial gnomon against the bells of Trinity College, ticking off each hour. But Trinity's bell is "always just a bit out of synchronization" with King's College bell. The wiki asks the right question: why? In 1664, no clock was accurate enough to keep perfect time, and the speed of sound alone (about 300 meters/second) means two bells 300 meters apart will sound a second apart to any listener. More importantly, "solar time" — what a sundial measures — drifts against "clock time" by up to 15 minutes depending on the season (the Equation of Time). Newton is recording data with an instrument (the bells) that introduces systematic error. For someone who would later tackle the [longitude problem](/topic/longitude), this is an early encounter with the maddening gap between measuring time and knowing it.
