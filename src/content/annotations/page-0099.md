[Drake Waterhouse](/topic/drake-waterhouse)'s apocalyptic convictions come into focus as the plague year coincides with his prophetic calculations.

**"End of days"** — [Drake Waterhouse](/topic/drake-waterhouse) bred and named his son Daniel for the mathematically calculated coming [Apocalypse](/topic/apocalypticism) in 1666. Many [Puritans](/topic/puritans) believed the year 1666 was prophetically significant because of Revelation 13:18, which identifies 666 as the Number of the Beast. Drake took this literally: the year would bring the end of days. When the [Great Plague](/topic/plague) struck in 1665, followed by the [Great Fire](/topic/great-fire-of-london) in 1666, it must have seemed like confirmation — except that the world stubbornly refused to end. The emotional fallout from this failed prophecy shapes Daniel's entire intellectual trajectory away from his father's millenarianism and toward [natural philosophy](/topic/natural-philosophy).
