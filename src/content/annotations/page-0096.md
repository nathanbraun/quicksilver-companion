Daniel and Isaac navigate plague-stricken London, moving through a city emptied by disease and Puritan fatalism.

**"YOU AND I ARE BUT EARTH"** — This inscription on a piece of crockery struck the original wiki annotator as "entirely too kooky to be fiction." It's consistent with [Puritan](/topic/puritans) material culture, which favored memento mori reminders on everyday objects — plates, cups, and embroidered samplers bearing skull motifs and reminders of mortality. The sentiment echoes Genesis 3:19: "for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return."
