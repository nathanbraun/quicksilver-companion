Young Ben is identified obliquely.

**"Ben. Son of Josaiah."** — Benjamin Franklin, of course — seven years old in 1713. His father was Josiah Franklin, a tallow chandler (candle maker) who had emigrated from Northamptonshire. Stephenson doesn't name-drop; he lets the reader figure it out. The device works because Franklin is one of the few colonial Americans a modern reader will recognize from minimal clues, and the slow reveal turns a street urchin into a historical figure mid-sentence.
