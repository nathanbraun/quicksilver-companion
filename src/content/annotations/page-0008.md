Young Ben Franklin gives Enoch an impromptu history lesson about European politics.

**Barkers** — The [Puritan](/topic/puritans) sect associated with Daniel Waterhouse's family. Stephenson's own annotation: "This is a fictitious sect that I invented, so there is no point in going and looking for more information about them. There were plenty of real ones, such as Diggers, Levellers, Ranters, Quakers, etc."

**King Carlos the Sufferer** — Charles II of Spain (1661-1700), the last Spanish Habsburg. Physically disabled, intellectually impaired, the product of generations of inbreeding. Everyone knew he'd never produce an heir. When he died and named [Louis XIV](/topic/louis-xiv)'s grandson as successor, it triggered the War of the Spanish Succession (1702-1713) — the geopolitical backdrop of the entire novel. The [Treaty of Utrecht](/topic/treaty-of-utrecht) ending that war was signed in 1713, the year this chapter takes place. Ben's little history lesson is Stephenson's way of sketching the world the reader has just entered.
