Isaac Barrow is mentioned, and Stephenson recommends a book.

**Isaac Barrow** — Newton's teacher at Cambridge, holder of the Lucasian Chair of Mathematics (which he resigned in Newton's favor in 1669). Barrow recognized Newton's genius before anyone else did. Stephenson's annotation: "As Huygens, Barrow, Newton, and [Hooke](/topic/robert-hooke) have all now been mentioned, this is as good a place as any to exhort people to buy the book *Huygens and Barrow, Newton & Hooke: Pioneers in mathematical analysis and catastrophe theory from evolvents to quasicrystals* by V.I. Arnol'd. Unfortunately for me I did not discover this remarkable little volume until after 99% of the Baroque Cycle had been written." A rare moment of an author wishing he'd found a source sooner.
