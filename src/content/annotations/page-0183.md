The Royal Society in session — a mixture of brilliance, absurdity, and political maneuvering.

**"Meeting of the Society"** — Stephenson's annotation: "These approximately three pages are a mixture of fictional material (basically anything to do with Waterhouse, Gunfleet, or [Louis Upnor](/topic/louis-anglesey-earl-of-upnor)) with fragments culled from the records of real [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society) meetings of this era." The Society's actual minutes survive in near-complete form and record an astonishing range of experiments, demonstrations, and debates. Meetings were held weekly at Gresham's College, with [Robert Hooke](/topic/robert-hooke) performing experiments and [Henry Oldenburg](/topic/henry-oldenburg) reading correspondence from scientists across Europe. Stephenson weaves fictional characters into these real proceedings, making Daniel a participant in historical events.
