John Wilkins's legacy casts a long shadow over the narrative — and over a small chronological puzzle.

**"John Wilkins..."** — The original wiki annotator points out that though this chapter is set in 1673, [John Wilkins](/topic/john-wilkins) died in November 1672. This could be a deliberate choice by Stephenson — the novel occasionally adjusts timelines for narrative purposes — or it could be an error. Wilkins was one of the founders of the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society), Bishop of Chester, author of *The Discovery of a World in the Moone* (1638), and the man behind the "Real Character" project to create a [universal language](/topic/universal-characteristic). His death left a hole in the Society's leadership and in the broader project of systematizing knowledge that he, more than anyone, had championed.
