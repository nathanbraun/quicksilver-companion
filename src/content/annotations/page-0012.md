Daniel Waterhouse's ship is introduced.

**Minerva** — The merchant vessel that will carry Daniel across the Atlantic. A three-masted barquentine retrofitted with rot-resistant Malabar teak, captained by the [pirate](/topic/pirates)-hating Captain van Hoek. Among its unusual features: a ship's wheel (still a novelty — tillers were standard), double spiral staircases to prevent crew collisions during battle, and a literate, numerate international crew. The ship is a floating showcase of early 18th-century cutting-edge technology.

**The name** — Minerva is the Roman equivalent of Athena: goddess of wisdom, strategic warfare, and craft. In the novel, Stephenson often pairs Minerva with [Mercury](/topic/quicksilver-mercury) (Quicksilver). Wisdom and speed — or, if you prefer, the static and the volatile. Daniel's voyage aboard the *Minerva* provides the 1713 frame narrative for the entire first book.
