Newton's secret identity as an alchemist surfaces through a Latin pseudonym.

**"Jeova Sanctus Unus"** — [Newton](/topic/isaac-newton) used this Latin phrase — meaning "God's Holy One" — as a pseudonym for his [alchemical](/topic/alchemy) writings. It is also an anagram of the Latin form of his name, *Isaacus Neutonus*. Newton's alchemy was not a casual hobby: he wrote over a million words on the subject and conducted experiments for decades. He kept this work secret because alchemy was disreputable even by 17th-century standards — it was associated with fraud, occultism, and the pursuit of forbidden knowledge. The pseudonym reveals Newton's characteristic blend of intellectual audacity and paranoid secrecy: he was deeply engaged in a pursuit he couldn't publicly acknowledge, signing his work with an anagram that was simultaneously a theological claim about his own divine mission.
