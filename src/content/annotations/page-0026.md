Enoch brews a strange drink.

**"Cha, or chai, or the, or tay"** — All real names for tea in different languages, reflecting its spread along different trade routes. "Cha" (Cantonese/Portuguese), "chai" (Hindi/Persian/Russian), "the/tay" (from the Hokkien Chinese *te*, which gave us the Dutch *thee* and English *tea*). In 1655, tea was essentially unknown in England. The first recorded English tea advertisement appeared in 1658: "That excellent and by all Physitians approved China Drink, called by the Chineans Tcha, by other nations Tay, alias Tee." Enoch is three years ahead of the market — one more small detail suggesting his connections extend far beyond provincial Grantham.
