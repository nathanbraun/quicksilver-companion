Jack Shaftoe navigates the winter streets of Paris, living among Armenian merchants and observing the city's complex social and financial hierarchies as news of a royal death arrives from England.

**"selling themselves as galley-slaves, just to get warm"** — In 17th-century France, being sentenced to the [galleys](/topic/galley-slaves) was a brutal punishment, though the extreme poverty of the era meant the state-provided sustenance of a slave was sometimes preferable to freezing.


**"sailed the first ship of coffee, ever, into Marseille in 1644"** — [Marseille](/topic/coffee-trade-marseille) was the primary entry point for coffee into France from the Levant, with the first commercial shipments arriving in the mid-17th century.


**"nuts caught in the mangle of the Thirty Years’ War"** — A devastating series of conflicts in Central Europe (1618–1648), the [Thirty Years' War](/topic/thirty-years-war) involved most European powers and resulted in millions of deaths.


**"family in Isfahan"** — The capital of the Safavid Empire in Persia, [Isfahan](/topic/isfahan) was a major center of global trade and home to a significant Armenian merchant community.


**"Place Dauphine, which was the sharp downstream tip of the Île de la Cité"** — A public square in Paris completed under Henry IV, [Place Dauphine](/topic/place-dauphine) was a center of commerce and public spectacles located near the Pont Neuf.


**"spang in the center of the Pont-Neuf"** — The [Pont-Neuf](/topic/pont-neuf) was the oldest standing bridge across the Seine and the social heart of the city, famous for its bustling street life and lack of houses.


**"St. Germain des Pres, outside the walls: a large quadrangle of monks’ residences"** — A powerful Benedictine monastery, the [Abbey of Saint-Germain-des-Prés](/topic/st-germain-des-pres) sat outside the city walls; its surrounding district became a fashionable site for the famous Saint-Germain Fair.


**"cross the green before Les Invalides—surrounded by its own wall and moat"** — [Les Invalides](/topic/les-invalides) was a complex initiated by Louis XIV in 1670 as a home and hospital for aged and unwell soldiers.


**"which, in those pre-Martinet days, basically meant counting them"** — [Jean Martinet](/topic/jean-martinet) was a French drillmaster who revolutionized the army with rigid discipline; his name became a synonym for a strict disciplinarian.


**"spent some time campaigning under John Churchill, alongside the French, in Germany"** — [John Churchill](/topic/john-churchill) was a brilliant English general who served under the French before becoming their greatest adversary during the War of the Spanish Succession.


**"southern part of the Marais, near the river"** — [The Marais](/topic/the-marais) was an aristocratic district of Paris known for its high concentration of "hôtels particuliers" (grand townhouses) built by the nobility.


**"statue of King Looie as Roman general"** — [Louis XIV](/topic/louis-xiv), the "Sun King," utilized Roman iconography to project his absolute monarchy and authority over France.


**"trading-courts at Leipzig and the Stock Exchange at Amsterdam"** — Established in 1602, the [Amsterdam Stock Exchange](/topic/amsterdam-stock-exchange) is considered the oldest modern securities market, reflecting Dutch financial power.


**"Four horses, all white as clouds, save for pink eyes and mottled hooves"** — The description of [albino horses](/topic/albino-horses) refers to a rare genetic trait; in this era, such horses were highly prized status symbols for royalty.


**"Jack proceeded crosstown to Les Halles"** — Known as "the belly of Paris," [Les Halles](/topic/les-halles) was the traditional central market and a chaotic hub of commerce for centuries.


**"Emmerdeur"** — A French vulgarity roughly translating to "troublemaker," though in this context, the [Emmerdeur](/topic/emmerdeur) refers to Jack's disguise as a "shitter" covered in filth.


**"one of the latter began to play accompanying music on a cornemuse"** — The [cornemuse](/topic/cornemuse) is a type of traditional French bagpipe common in folk music and street performances during the Baroque period.


**"the Lieutenant of Police could scan a torrent of people"** — This refers to the [Lieutenant General of Police](/topic/lieutenant-general-of-police), an office created by Louis XIV in 1667 to modernize urban order, lighting, and surveillance in Paris.


**"bills of exchange"** — A precursor to modern paper money, [bills of exchange](/topic/bills-of-exchange) allowed merchants to transfer large sums across borders without carrying physical gold or silver.


**"maneuvered round the trundling manure-carts all the way to the Bastille"** — [The Bastille](/topic/the-bastille) was a fortress that served as a state prison and armory, standing as a symbol of royal authority at the city's eastern edge.


**"To Lyons was a long journey, almost all the way to Italy"** — In the 17th century, [Lyon](/topic/lyon-banking-center) was a major European banking and silk-trading hub linking French and Italian financial markets.


**"The countryside was divided up into innumerable separate pays with their own tolls"** — Pre-Revolutionary France was divided into [pays](/topic/french-internal-customs) with complex internal customs barriers that significantly slowed domestic trade.


**"headed for Genoa and Marseilles"** — [Genoese bankers](/topic/genoese-bankers) operated a sophisticated private postal and intelligence network that often moved news faster than official state channels.
