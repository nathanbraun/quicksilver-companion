A reference to Jewish communities in Cambridge's orbit.

**Ashkenazi** — The Ashkenazim are one of the two major Jewish ethnic groups (the other being Sephardim). Ashkenazi Jews are generally of Central and Eastern European descent, tracing their communities to medieval Germany and France before migrating eastward into Poland, Lithuania, and Russia. The distinction matters for the novel because Cromwell had readmitted Jews to England in 1656 (page 21), and the first to arrive were mostly Sephardic — wealthy traders from the Amsterdam community, expelled generations earlier from Spain and Portugal. Ashkenazi Jews would arrive in larger numbers later. In the 1660s, Jews in England were still a tiny, legally precarious minority, and their presence near Cambridge would have been notable.
