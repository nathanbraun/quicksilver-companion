Daniel meets his roommate at Trinity College.

**"Isaac was pale and starlight"** — Daniel's first glimpse of Newton. The real Newton was born premature and so small his mother said "he might have been put into a quart mug." No one expected him to survive infancy. He attended grammar school at Grantham but performed poorly in his prescribed studies, showing interest only in mechanical things — waterwheels, clocks, sundials. He lasted two years before his mother pulled him out to run the family farm (he was terrible at that too). His uncle and schoolmaster eventually persuaded his mother to send him to Cambridge. The pale, odd, intensely private young man Daniel encounters is historically accurate — Newton had almost no friends before or after Cambridge.
