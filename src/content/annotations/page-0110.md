London's geography comes alive as Daniel moves through the plague-era city.

**"Walbrook"** — The Walbrook is one of London's hidden rivers, a tributary of the Thames that once flowed openly through the city. By the 1660s it was already partly covered over, but its course still defined the local topography and street layout. After the [Great Fire of 1666](/topic/great-fire-of-london), much of its remaining open course was bricked over during [rebuilding](/topic/restoration-london). Today it flows entirely underground, one of London's many "lost rivers." The name survives in Walbrook street, near the Bank of England — a reminder that London's geography is layered with invisible waterways.
