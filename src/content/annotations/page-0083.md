Isaac tries to convert Daniel to a Platonic view of reality, using Wilkins's system.

**"Translated into the Analytical Language"** — Isaac uses [John Wilkins](/topic/john-wilkins)'s universal "Analytical Language" — a system that classifies all knowledge into categories and assigns each a unique symbol — to argue for Platonic idealism: the idea that abstract forms are more real than physical objects. Daniel, a hardened empiricist, isn't buying it. The wiki frames this as Plato's Cave vs. Epicurus's Garden — the ancient debate between those who believe reality is an imperfect shadow of perfect forms (Plato) and those who believe the physical world is all there is (Epicurus). Isaac is Team Plato. Daniel is Team Epicurus. This philosophical split will deepen over decades and eventually mirror the Newton-[Leibniz](/topic/gottfried-wilhelm-von-leibniz) divide: Newton seeking God's perfect mathematical laws behind nature, Leibniz building practical machines to compute with.
