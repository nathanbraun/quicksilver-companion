A Royal Society meeting brings the novel's cast of natural philosophers together.

**"Ned Ward"** — Stephenson's annotation: "This is one of a series of satirical sketches that Ward wrote about various clubs of London. He calls this one the 'Vertuoso's Club' but it is obviously the [Royal Society](/topic/royal-society)." Edward "Ned" Ward (1667–1731) was a London publican and satirist who wrote scathing, often vulgar accounts of London life. His sketch of the "Vertuoso's Club" portrays the Royal Society's members as eccentric buffoons obsessed with trivia — weighing air, measuring the growth of fingernails, debating whether spiders can hear music. Ward's satire captures how natural philosophers looked to outsiders: grown men performing bizarre experiments with dead earnest seriousness.
