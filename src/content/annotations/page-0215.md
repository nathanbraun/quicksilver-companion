A famous Pepys anecdote makes its way into the novel.

**"The dog shit in the boat"** — Stephenson's annotation: "A well-known anecdote told by [Pepys](/topic/samuel-pepys), who witnessed it." The diary entry for May 25, 1660 records a moment aboard the ship carrying Charles II back to England for the Restoration. The king's dog defecated on the boat deck — a moment of comic bathos amid the solemn pageantry of a monarch's return from exile. Pepys recorded it with his usual eye for the undignified detail that makes grand occasions human. Stephenson uses moments like these to puncture the mythologized version of history and show these figures as real, embodied people.
