Charles II's cruel wit and the fate of former Puritans under the Restoration.

**"[Knott Bolstrood](/topic/knott-bolstrood)"** — The footnote tells us that the king gave Bolstrood the title Lord Penistone so he would be unable to write his name without writing "penis." This is characteristic of [Charles II](/topic/charles-ii)'s humor — vindictive, juvenile, and politically calculated. Stephenson's annotation: "This turn in the career of the fictitious Knott Bolstrood, as well as other things that happen to him later, were loosely suggested by events in the life of Shaftesbury." Anthony Ashley Cooper, 1st Earl of Shaftesbury, was a [Puritan](/topic/puritans)-sympathizing politician who served in the CABAL ministry before turning against the king over the question of Catholic succession. Bolstrood's humiliation reflects the precarious position of former Parliamentarians and Puritans under the Restoration — tolerated, even useful, but never safe from royal caprice.
