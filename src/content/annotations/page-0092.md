The chapter ends mid-sentence.

**"The page ends abruptly"** — A reader on the wiki asked if this was a printing error. Stephenson himself replied: "It's intended to be read continuously from the bottom of p. 92 to the top of 93, hence: 'all of a sudden it's The Plague Year.' Unfortunately the footnote breaks it up a bit." The abrupt break is a deliberate structural device — the *Minerva* chapters in 1713 keep getting interrupted by flashbacks to Daniel's past, and here Stephenson makes the interruption physical. You're on a ship, then suddenly you're in London during the [plague](/topic/plague). The disorientation is the point.
