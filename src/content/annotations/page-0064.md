Back in 1713, Daniel is shocked by his nephew's theology, and a familiar Boston figure appears.

**"A Free Will man?"** — Daniel is stunned that his young nephew Wait Still Waterhouse embraces free will — the idea that human choices are genuinely "up to us" rather than predetermined by God. For a Waterhouse, this is heresy. The [Puritan](/topic/puritans) Calvinist tradition held predestination as bedrock: God chose the elect before creation, and no human action could change it. The free will vs. predestination debate was one of the deepest fractures in Protestant theology, splitting Calvinists from Arminians. That a Waterhouse in Boston has gone Arminian tells Daniel how much the world has changed in his lifetime.

**Mrs. Goose** — Stephenson's annotation: "There was a Mrs. Goose in Boston. Her gravestone stands in the Granary Burying Ground. It is by no means clear that she is THE Mrs. Goose responsible for the Mother Goose stories. Other people, who lived earlier, seem to have a claim on that honor." A characteristic Stephenson move — dropping a real historical figure into the background without fanfare.
