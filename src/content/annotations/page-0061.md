Newton grooms Daniel's hair — an unsettling intimacy.

**"A certain natural wave"** — Isaac combs and styles Daniel's hair, finding a "natural wave" that satisfies him. The scene is deliberately strange. Newton, who had essentially zero social skills, is performing an act that in 17th-century England carried erotic overtones — [Samuel Pepys](/topic/samuel-pepys)'s biographer Claire Tomalin notes that brushing hair was considered a seductive act. But Newton's motives are probably more clinical than romantic: he was fascinated by patterns, curves, and the physical properties of everything he touched. The wiki speculates he may have been thinking about topology — the "hairy ball theorem" concerns whether hair on a sphere can be combed flat (it can't). Whether Newton was sublimating mathematics into grooming or simply asserting social dominance over his new roommate, Daniel tolerates it — wisely sensing that this is not someone you want to alienate.
