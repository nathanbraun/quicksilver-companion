The scene shifts to 1655 Grantham. Young Isaac Newton lives with the apothecary Clarke.

**Clarke** — Stephenson's own annotation: "Clarke really existed, and Newton really lived with him. The account here is mostly fictionalized but stiffened here and there with historical evidence. See Richard Westfall's *Never at Rest*." Newton was sent to board with the Clarke family to attend the King's School, Grantham. The real Clarke was an apothecary — a fact that matters because Newton's early exposure to chemical processes in Clarke's shop may have planted the seed of his lifelong obsession with [alchemy](/topic/alchemy).
