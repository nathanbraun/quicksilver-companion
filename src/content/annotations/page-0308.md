A funeral reveals the Comstock family's entanglement with naval power and political ambition.

**"Richard Comstock"** — The original wiki annotator identifies a historical source: "The remark which Stephenson attributes here to [John Comstock](/topic/john-comstock) at Richard Comstock's funeral was in fact written by John Evelyn as a comment upon the death of Lord Sandwich." Lord Sandwich (Edward Montagu, 1st Earl) was killed at the Battle of Solebay in 1672 during the Third Anglo-Dutch War. His body was found floating in the sea, identifiable only by his clothes. Stephenson frequently repurposes real historical details — relocating them to fictional characters while preserving their emotional truth. The Comstock family, like the novel's other fictional dynasties, is a composite of several real Restoration families.
