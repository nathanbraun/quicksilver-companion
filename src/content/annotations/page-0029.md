Young Isaac Newton gets into a fight at school.

**"Rub his prisoner's face"** — Stephenson's own annotation: "The fight and the nose-rubbing are described in Westfall's *Never at Rest*, pp. 59-60. He traces the story to Keynes who got it from Newton's relative and early biographer Conduitt." Stephenson clarifies that at least one early reader thought the other boy was terribly mutilated — "This was not the case." Newton is pre-adolescent and can only do so much damage in the few moments before the schoolmaster intervenes. The real historical incident, though, was pivotal: after winning the fight, Newton resolved to beat the boy academically too, and rose from near-bottom of the class to the top.

**Lace** — Newton recreates needle lace to net an apple. The original wiki saw DNA symbolism here (the double helix), which may be a stretch, but Newton's childhood obsession with making things — water clocks, sundials, miniature windmills — is well documented. The boy who would invent [calculus](/topic/calculus-priority-dispute) and decode the universe started by reverse-engineering crafts.
